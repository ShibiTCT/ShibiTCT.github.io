Glossary of Terminology

The terminology listed on this page is used commonly within the assistance dog community and/or within organisations. Most of these are informal and many have alternatives. There can be conflicting definitions as there isn't always consistency between mulitple organisations and the community.Some of the phrases listed may have different meanings internationally. The main difference is between the UK and USA. You can read more about the differences between the two countries assistance dog world here.

Assistance Dog/AD

A dog trained to mitagate the disability of an individual. There are many different types of assistance dog including guide dogs, psychiatric assistance dogs, hearing dogs and medical alert dogs.

Service Dog

A service dog is a dog that is trained and used within the emergency services or army. There may be exceptions to this for example the organisation Service dogs UK which trains PTSD assistance dogs.

Emotional Support Animal/Emotional Support Dog/ESA/ESD

An informal term which describes a pet that gives emotional support. They are not recognised legally as anything more than a pet. You can read more about ESAs here

Assistance Dog in Training/ADiT

A dog that is training to be an assistance dog. Usually dogs labelled like this have completed basic training , however, this is not always the case. These dogs do not automatically have public access rights and have to ask permission before accessing non-pet friendly places. Please be aware that there is a unclear boundary within law on when an ADiT becomes recognised as an AD.

Assistance Dog Prospect/Puppy

A term used within the community to describe a dog which has the potential to become an assistance dogs. Usually these puppies will have been temperament tested but will be in early steps of there training. This is usually basic dog training and socialisation. Puppies described as prospects are usually owned by the Disabled person and are being owner trained or aided-owner trained whilst Assistance dog puppies are usually a programmes dogs that have been given to puppy walkers/raisers for socialisation and early training. This is not a strict rule and some will use either term.

Support Dog

A term sometimes used to describe Assistance dogs, usually those trained by the organisation Support Dogs. They term is sometimes used to also describe a Companion and Family Therapy Dogs.

Family Therapy Dogs/Family Dog/Buddy Dog

A term used to describe a dog that usually has some basic training which provides a theraputic presence usually for a Disabled child and are often career changed dogs. These dogs are not assistance dogs and do not have public access rights. They can also be used for Animal Assisted Therapy.

Companion Dog

A dog that is has a that usually has some basic training and provides a theraputic presence usually for a Disabled adult. These dogs are often career changed dogs and do not have public access rights

Therapy Dog

A dog that has been trained to be a theraputic presence for multiple people and goes to places where they may be needed such as schools,hospitals,airports and care home. Usually these dogs come from charities such as PAT and are volunteers pets.

Facility Dog

A therapy dog which usually only works in one place and has been trained for a specific environment

Washed/Career Changed Assistance Dog

A dog which was not able to complete assistance dog training. This can be due to health or behavioural issues though 'career changed' may also be used for bitches which have been selected for a breeding programme or a dog that changes type of assistance dog traing (eg. a guide dog that becomes a mobility dog). 'Washed' is more commonly used within the community whilst 'Career changed' is more common within organisations.
