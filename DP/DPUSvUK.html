USA v UK

The USA is a big part of the assistance dog world, with around half a million assistance dogs calling the US home. A side effect of this is that US-specific terminology sometimes ends up getting used within the UK. Here are some of the differences between the two countries.

Assistance Dog

Within the UK, an assistance dog is a dog trained to help a Disabled individual. In the USA, assistance dog is used often as an informal term ecompassing ESAs,service dogs and sometimes therapy or facility dogs

Service Animal

In the UK, a service animal is a animal that works for the emergency services or the armed forces. In the USA, it is a dog or minature horse that is task trained to assist a disabled person.

Emotional Support Animals

An Emotional Support Animal is an animal that provides emotional support or comfort to an individual. For more infotmationsee ESAs.
